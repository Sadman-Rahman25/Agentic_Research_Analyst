## TL;DR
Modern multi-agent LLM systems handle disagreement between agents through various approaches, including understanding why disagreements arise [1], cross-verification among agents [2], and decentralized architectures that preserve data privacy [3][7]. These systems can efficiently handle complex tasks by working together [15] and can be used in real-world applications such as software development, financial trading, robotics, and enterprise operations [18].

## Background
Multi-agent LLM systems are designed to enable collaborative model training and decision-making among multiple agents. These systems face challenges such as handling disagreements between agents, preserving data privacy, and improving overall learning performance. Understanding how these systems handle disagreements is crucial for their effective deployment in various applications.

## Key Findings
Current approaches to handling disagreement between agents in modern multi-agent LLM systems include understanding why disagreements arise [1]. Cross-verification among agents can effectively overcome disagreements in multi-agent collaborative filtering frameworks [2]. Decentralized Multi-agent Learning (DML) enables collaborative model training while preserving data privacy [3][7]. However, inherent heterogeneity in agents' resources may lead to substantial variations in training time in DML [4][8]. Communication is an effective mechanism for coordinating the behaviors of multiple agents in MADRL [5], and agents can communicate various types of messages [6]. Training objectives, such as consensus-based or adversarial losses, can improve overall learning performance and achieve objectives in multi-agent deep reinforcement learning (MADRL) systems [9]. Multi-agent reinforcement learning models can enhance performance by leveraging sequential decision-making processes [11], and the order in which agents make decisions is important in multi-agent reinforcement learning [12].

## Open Questions / Gaps
While the findings provide insights into how modern multi-agent LLM systems handle disagreements between agents, there are still open questions and gaps in our understanding. For example, the impact of centralized vs decentralized architectures on disagreement handling in multi-agent LLM systems is not fully understood [8]. Additionally, the effectiveness of consensus-based or adversarial losses in shaping agent behavior in multi-agent LLM systems is still a topic of research [10]. Further investigation is needed to fully address these gaps and develop more effective multi-agent LLM systems.
