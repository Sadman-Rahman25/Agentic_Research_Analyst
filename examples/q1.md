## Background
Sparse retrieval uses term-based weighting for keyword search [2], whereas dense retrieval uses vector embeddings for semantic search [1]. Sparse retrieval methods, like TF-IDF or BM25, represent text as high-dimensional vectors where most dimensions are zero, encoding the presence or absence of specific words [3]. In contrast, dense retrieval encodes queries and documents into dense vectors (768–1536 dimensions) and ranks results using semantic similarity [1][4].
